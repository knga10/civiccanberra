ACT Budget at a Glance — 2026–27

Australian Capital Territory · Produced by the Chief Minister, Treasury and Economic Development Directorate Source: act.gov.au

Treasurer's Message — Chris Steel MLA

The 2026-27 Budget represents the most significant housing investment since self-government, aiming to grow homeownership opportunities for current and future Canberrans.

Key themes: - Stamp duty eliminated for first home buyers and unit-titled properties (supporting "missing middle" housing like terraces/townhouses) - Continued microeconomic and planning reforms to boost construction productivity - New "Canberra House" Pattern Book of pre-approved missing middle home designs - Halved Lease Variation Charge on missing middle projects - War in the Middle East has pressured the ACT economy via fuel/supply costs in H1 2026, but ACT economy remains resilient — 29+ years of continuous growth, lower inflation than national average, low unemployment - Cost-of-living relief targeted at food relief, school expenses, transport concessions, energy costs, housing crisis support - New Restructure Fund and limits on external contractor use to manage expenditure - Continued investment in health, education, transport infrastructure (adjusted for sustainability)

Budget Overview

  • Budget deficit 2026-27: $323.4 million → improving to a surplus of $244.2 million by 2028-29
  • Return to cash operating surplus projected in 2026-27
  • Housing commitments: 13,200 public housing dwellings by 2030; 30,000 more homes overall by 2030
  • New investments in emergency services, domestic/family/sexual violence prevention, community sector, health

Strengthening Housing Access & Supply

$770 million total for new and expanded housing initiatives.

Public housing

  • $360m+ for 450 additional public housing dwellings (toward 13,200 by 2030 / 5,000 additional public+community+affordable homes by 2030)
  • $90m+ capital funding for 1,000 property renewals + 400 net portfolio growth by June 2027
  • $180m+ to uplift repairs & maintenance (prioritising vacant property upgrades)
  • Progressive insourcing of public housing repairs/maintenance
  • Funding for crisis/transitional housing — up to 28 dwellings (Commonwealth National Housing Infrastructure Facility)
  • Support for 1,000+ affordable homes via Housing Australia Future Fund, incl. ~$650m in ACT government guarantees

Stamp duty & buyer support

  • First home buyers and eligible pensioners: no stamp duty
  • Owner-occupier off-the-plan unit concession made permanent + expanded to turn-key units
  • Disability Duty Concession Scheme: no duty regardless of price
  • Commercial stamp duty tax-free threshold raised to $2.1 million
  • Pensioner Duty Concession expanded to Service Pension recipients with permanent incapacity; DVA Gold Card holders — 12-month wait removed

Planning & supply

  • Planning studies for housing pipeline / developable sites, heritage regulatory functions under Heritage Act 2004
  • $1 million for the Canberra House Pattern Book (dual/tri-occupancy, terraces, townhouses)
  • Temporary 50% remission of Lease Variation Charge in RZ1/RZ2 zones (conditions: LVC not paid/deferred before 10 June 2026, DA approval before 30 June 2029, construction complete by 31 Dec 2030)

Headline figures: - $770m total new/expanded housing - $367.9m — new Public Housing Pipeline - $183.4m — public housing repairs & maintenance - Stamp duty eliminated for all first home buyers

Community Support, Social Inclusion & Safety

$182.6 million over 4 years in new initiatives, including: - $44.2m — frontline domestic, family & sexual violence (DFSV) services, secure long-term funding - $16.9m — housing and homelessness services - $16.7m — community legal assistance sector

Homelessness

  • New Housing Crisis Support Fund: up to 4 weeks' rent (capped at $2,500) + new $250 Crisis Support Payment for utilities/insurance/furnishings
  • $16m+ for providers: Samaritan House, Roadhouse, Bluedoor, Our Place Braddon, Community Housing Canberra
  • Housing Asset Assistance Program (HAAP) expanded by 65 properties; +10 properties under Housing First model

Other commitments

  • Release of ACT Domestic, Family and Sexual Violence Strategy 2026–2036
  • National Multicultural Festival, Gender Pathways Peer Navigation Service, free period products in public facilities
  • Funding for Canberra Community Law, Women's Legal Centre, Aboriginal Legal Service
  • Additional food relief / emergency material aid
  • Residential care, crisis accommodation, emergency supports for at-risk children/youth
  • Children and Young People Equipment Loan Scheme continuation
  • National Agreement on Foundational Supports — Thriving Kids
  • Bimberi Youth Justice Centre operations
  • Family Settlement Assistance Grants, Humanitarian Fund

Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Canberrans

  • $1.2m — culturally informed perinatal mental health "Safe Space" service
  • $1.9m — boost Aboriginal/Torres Strait Islander health workforce (IAHA Health Academy, 30 students/year)
  • $500,000 — support Yerrabi Yurwang's Housing Australia Future Fund application (29-dwelling Moncrieff townhouse development); plus $3m+ via Affordable Housing Project Fund
  • $754,000 — Truth Telling (Seed of Truth) Project (Uluru Statement)
  • $1.8m — justice system reform oversight, two independent justice advisors (Jumbunna review response), expanded Elected Body capacity

Health

$910 million+ over 4 years for public health care, mental health, community wellbeing, health infrastructure.

Northside Hospital

  • $1.3 billion over 7 years (brings total commitment to ~$1.5 billion over a decade)
  • 7-storey clinical services building, 300+ treatment spaces
  • Features: state-of-the-art ED with paediatric short-stay unit, 14-bed ICU, co-designed Birth Centre (6 birthing suites), 8 operating theatres (+2 future-proofed), 2 endoscopy suites (+1 future), 200+ new inpatient beds, expanded outpatient spaces
  • Main construction expected to start 2027-28 — largest single health infrastructure project in ACT history

Other health funding

  • $47.6m — breast/lung cancer services, paediatric orthopaedics, acute palliative care
  • $23.4m — new Acute Palliative Care Unit
  • $21.7m — new medical equipment
  • $14.1m — junior medical officer wellbeing/career development
  • Insourcing hospital cleaning staff at Canberra Hospital
  • South Tuggeranong Health Centre operations (opening late 2026)
  • Outpatient transport service for chronic conditions
  • Canberra Script (real-time prescription monitoring) upgrades
  • Additional funding for free abortion services
  • Voluntary assisted dying substitute decision-making reform exploration
  • Expansion of Autism Spectrum Disorder assessment services

Education & Training

  • $3m+ — free four-year-old preschool program (2026–2027) + early childhood education/care regulation
  • 2026 round of Future of Education Student Equity Fund; Head Start Program in 2027

  • Gas furnace → electric reverse-cycle replacements; feasibility studies for flexible education/specialist schools; additional school cleaning resourcing

Early childhood education ($14m+)

  • Extended Preschool Reform Agreement funding
  • Education Directorate regulatory obligations (Approved Provider, National Law)
  • ACT Children's Education and Care Assurance — assessments/audits capacity
  • Teacher Quality Institute — mandatory registration for early childhood teachers

Workforce (~$100 million)

  • UNSW Canberra City Campus establishment
  • Electric Vehicle TAFE Centre of Excellence + Clean Energy Fund (National Skills Agreement)
  • Fee-free TAFE extension — 9,093 additional training places (healthcare, advanced tech, construction, hospitality, foundation skills)

Jobs, Entertainment, Sport & Culture

$143.5 million over 4 years in new initiatives.

Belconnen Basketball Stadium expansion

  • $37.5 million — courts increase from 4 → 7 (75% increase)
  • Convertible show court with retractable seating for up to 1,800 spectators
  • New change rooms/toilets, increased parking, solar panels + batteries

Other initiatives

  • National Convention and Entertainment Centre — progressed to "construction ready" stage
  • $7m — Major Event Fund continuation/expansion
  • New ACT aquatic strategy + feasibility studies (Amaroo & Kambah District Playing Fields pavilions)
  • International cricket matches at Manuka (with Cricket Australia) + increased support for local women's sporting teams
  • Arts organisation funding increased 25% plus indexation (~$8m+)
  • Upgrades: Canberra Stadium, Exhibition Park, Manuka Oval, National Arboretum, Stromlo Forest Park

  • Screen Canberra continued funding (TV/film/digital gaming) + Canberra Business Advice and Support Service expansion

Transport, City Services, Environment & Climate

$343.1 million over 4 years in new initiatives.

Canberra–Sydney Rail

  • ACT investing $25 million (total project $100m: Commonwealth $50m + NSW $25m + ACT $25m)
  • Focus: priority rail infrastructure upgrades Canberra–Goulburn
  • Potential works: new express services, level crossing upgrades, track layout/switching improvements, station/train storage upgrades, planning for passing loops & track straightening

Local infrastructure & city services ($65m+)

  • Shopping centre upgrades: Kingston, Chisholm, Cooleman Court (Weston), Kippax
  • New public toilet — Umbagong District Park
  • Hall Village Main Active Travel Route (Hall Village → Gold Creek) + Belconnen–City public transport corridor design
  • New stormwater infrastructure for Hall Village
  • Bushfire risk reduction (fire trails, prescribed burns, vegetation management)
  • City services: mowing, street sweeping, horticulture

Climate & environment

  • Next Climate Change Strategy + first Action Plan
  • ACT Environment Grants Program expansion (community groups — climate resilience/biodiversity)
  • Renters' Home Energy Program & Home Energy Support Program continuation
  • Sustainable Household Scheme loan cap increased to $20,000 (from 1 July 2026); electric cargo bikes added as eligible
  • Specialised biosecurity team funding

Water

  • Household tapware replacement, sportsground irrigation upgrades, business/school/government water management
  • Consumer education + regulatory review for new-build water efficiency
  • Lake Tuggeranong water quality measures

Public transport

  • Continued weekday flexible transport services + incentives for weekend driver shifts + Woden Bus Depot operations support
  • Reduced monthly fare cap for tertiary students
  • Light Rail Stage 2A (City–Commonwealth Park) continued delivery toward Woden

Wellbeing

  • Third consecutive budget continuing the six wellbeing priorities framework
  • Wellbeing approach embeds social, environmental, and community factors alongside economic growth in budget decisions
  • Focus: intergenerational equity, cross-agency challenges, early supports, critical services, transformational infrastructure
